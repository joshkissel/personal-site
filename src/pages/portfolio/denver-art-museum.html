An Identity Case Study

FREDERIC C. HAMILTON BUILDING

Opened October 2006

In 2006 the Frederic C. Hamilton building was opened to the public and has become an iconic Denver landmark as well as an icon for the Denver Art Museum. I tasked myself to bring the brand inline with the architectural and environmental design. In the hopes that it would unify the design experince.

Denver Art Museum

The Current Mark

This is the current logo for the Denver Art Musuem. It left me with a few questions. Why two typefaces? Why is Denver sans-serif and Art Museum serif? Where does the green come from? Is this mark identifyable or memerable? Now, I have answered these questions for myself but they are more based on assumption or opinion so I'll leave it there.

Snow Problem!

Every Snowflake is Different, Right?

The snowflake is the center of the ski. However, the ski binding will obscure most of this pattern. The rest of the ski is mirrored on both sides of the binding so you don't miss out on any other ugly bit.

Seneca Skis

These ski (topsheet) graphics were created, as a concept, for Seneca Skis based out of Bozeman,MT. The "S" seen on this part of the ski is a replication of Senca's logo. This was probably one of the most time consuming pattern pieces to create.

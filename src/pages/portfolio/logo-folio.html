Zócalo

Discover the Source

This was also a school group project which involved a true client. Our group met with a local coffee shop to learn their design pain points and to see where they envisioned their design. Zócalo is a Spanish term for the city square. The logo mark, which was my responsibilty, incorporates a map pin, a (city) square, and a mountain silhouette.

New Haven and Hartford Railroad

Updating the Original

This was a two part project. I wanted to rebrand the railroad in two ways. The first rebrand was to use as much of the orignal logo (top left) as possible.

New Haven Railroad

Going Places

This was the second piece of the rebrand. I wanted to make as contemporary of a mark as possible. Yet, I was still able to use lettering from the original mark which makes both the "N" and the "H". Additionally, I created the "Going Places" tagliine to help reinvigorate the brand.

Studio Marka

The Name Brand

This was a student branding agency. The work "Marka" translates roughly to brand in a few languages. It's also a play on a brand "mark" nomenclature. The tagline "The Name Brand" then starts to take on multiple meanings. Really, the entire messaging is a giant play on words.
